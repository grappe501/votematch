## Title

**VoteMatch**

## Subtitle

Spreadsheet-to-voter-file matching and review tools for petition signature tracking.

---

## What it does

VoteMatch helps teams **align petition signer rows with voter-file records** and **track counts** for initiative work. Matching produces **match confidence** scores and flags rows that need **review**—separate from any legal determination.

## Confidence scoring

Rows receive **match confidence** (a technical match strength), not a statement of **legal validity**. Lower scores mean **review required**, not that a signature is “bad.”

## Review workflow

Operators can work through a **review queue** for rows that need attention (for example, non-matches, lower confidence, or duplicate flags). **Review required** means more information or a decision is needed—not a conclusion about eligibility.

## Local and private reporting

Sensitive spreadsheets and voter extracts should be handled **locally or in a secured environment**, not on the public website. **Reporting** (for example ward or county summaries) is intended for **private** operator use alongside your own procedures.

## Ward and county summaries

Where your data supports it, tools can summarize activity **by ward** or **by county** for reporting. Geography labels follow your configured initiative scope.

## Built for initiative tracking

VoteMatch is built to support **administrative** initiative and petition tracking: matching, review status, and rollups—without campaign messaging on the tool itself.

## Nonvoter review file

When no voter match is found after review, rows may be placed in a **nonvoter review file** for record-keeping. That step is an **organizational** action, not a legal finding.

---

## Disclaimer

**VoteMatch helps organize and review voter-file matches. It does not determine legal sufficiency or eligibility by itself.**
